AFRICAN AUTHORS

1. Rosina Acheampong is a Ghanaian educationist. She was the first female deputy director general of the Ghana Education Service and a former headmistress of Wesley Girls High School.

On 13 April 2019, she was awarded with a lifetime achievement award for her contribution to girl-child education in the country by Glitz Africa at the Labadi Beach Hotel.

She gained admission into Wesley Girls’ High School in 1954 and there she had her secondary education. She later furthered at the University of Ghana and the University of Cape Coast where she acquired a bachelor's degree in French and a professional course (PGCE) respectively.

2. Ama Ata Aidoo, née Christina Ama Aidoo is a Ghanaian author, poet, playwright and academic. She was the Minister of Education under the Jerry Rawlings administration. Just like Rosie, Ama Ata also attended the ever green all girls' school on the Kakumdu hills Wesley Girls’ High School .

In 2000, she established the Mbaasem Foundation to promote and support the work of African women writers.

A few of Ama's books written over the years include:

  • Changes: A love Story
  • Dilemma of a Ghost
  • Our sister Killjoy
  • No sweetness here and other stories
